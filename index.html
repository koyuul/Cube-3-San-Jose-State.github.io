About

Cube3 is a CubeSat club at San Jose State University. Cube3 teaches members about the subsystems involved in cube satellites through projects and technical workshops. There are two ongoing cube satellite projects called 7Up-Sat and 9-Sat.

Our Current Project

At the start of summer 2021, Cube3 took on the Nodes Project. The goal of the nodes project is to create a network of sensors that will be air-dropped near the Mexican stratovolcano Popocatépetl. The sensors will monitor volcanic activity in order to notify nearby cities of potential eruptions.

High Level Overview

Popocatépetl is roughly 18,000 ft. in elevation and is the second largest volcano in North America. Popocatépetl is a great opportunity for Cube3 as it allows us to demonstrate a CubeSat-like system. There are various principles we are able to apply to this project such being able to communicate long distances over LoRa, building a device that can withstand extreme environments, and have the power ability to carry out a long term mission spanning several months.

7Up-Sat

Small satellite that fits within a can sized cylinder. This is the recommended project for people new to programming or embedded systems. This group overviews the basics of cube satellites in order to get a more holistic idea of the project. Intended to be launched in conjunction with the Rocketry club on a class 1 or 2 rocket.

9-Sat

A 3U cube satellite prototype that will house a hyper-spectral imaging camera. This project requires a lot of knowledge of cube satellites. Most of the parts must be researched prior to purchasing in order to complete the project effectively. Only recommended for those familiar with satellites or strong embedded systems knowledge.
